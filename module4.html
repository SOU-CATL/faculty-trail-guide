MODULE 4

The Scenic Overlook

Visual Signposting in Moodle

The Trail Metaphor

Every Overlook, Labeled the Same Way

On a well-marked trail, every overlook is labeled the same way. You know where you are because the signs are consistent. In Moodle, visual signposting works the same way — consistent headers, icons, and structure so students don't waste cognitive energy figuring out where the assignment lives.

The UDL Connection

Reducing Cognitive Load Through Consistency

Executive function support is about reducing cognitive load. When every module in your course looks different — different headings, different file names, different structure — students with ADHD, autism, or anxiety spend mental energy on navigation instead of learning.

Consistent visual rhythm means:

  • Students can find what they need without asking
  • The course feels predictable and safe
  • They can focus on the content, not on decoding the interface

Who benefits most: Students with ADHD (predictability reduces overwhelm), autism spectrum (consistency lowers anxiety), anxiety disorders, and students managing multiple courses simultaneously.

Your Trail Activity

Mark Your Course Consistently

1
Choose a consistent set of icons or headers for your course (use the provided icon set or design your own).
2
Apply visual signposting to at least three Moodle modules:
  • Same header style for "This Week's Readings"
  • Same icon for "This Week's Activity"
  • Same visual cue for "Submit Here"
3
Standardize your file naming convention across modules.

Example: Week3_Readings_Article1.pdf — not final FINAL article (1).pdf

🎒 Bonus: Run the "student test." Look at your course as if you're a student who has never taken it. Can you find the assignment in under 30 seconds?
