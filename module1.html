MODULE 1

The Shared Map

Social Annotation with Hypothes.is

The Trail Metaphor

Leaving Cairns for Each Other

A dense reading is like a forest with no trail markers. When students annotate together, they leave cairns for each other — pointing to the key ideas, flagging the confusing passages, marking where the trail gets hard. Hypothes.is is your cairn-building tool.

The UDL Connection

Why This Supports Neurodiverse Learners

Social annotation supports neurodivergent learners because it:

  • Breaks up passive, isolated reading into guided, interactive engagement
  • Allows asynchronous participation — no real-time performance pressure
  • Makes thinking visible without requiring verbal fluency
  • Reduces the "read everything, then discuss" cognitive load
  • Lets students engage with text at their own pace and comfort level

Who benefits most: Students with ADHD, dyslexia, anxiety, processing differences, and those who think best in writing or fragments.

Your Trail Activity

Replace One Discussion Thread

Replace one discussion thread with a Hypothes.is annotation activity. Three options:

Option A — Anchored Text Discussion

Students annotate a shared article using one of three lenses:

  • Clarify: "This part is confusing because..."
  • Connect: "This reminds me of..."
  • Extend: "An example from my teaching or discipline..."

Option B — Video Annotation

Share a video link through Hypothes.is. Students annotate at specific timestamps with discussion questions or responses.

Option C — Artifact Meta-Overview (Group Experience)

Students annotate a discipline-specific artifact — a case study, policy document, or primary source — as a collaborative group exercise.
