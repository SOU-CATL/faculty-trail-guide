The Trailhead Kiosk

Gemini Syllabus Splash Page

The Trail Metaphor

A Clear, Welcoming Sign at the Start

Every good trail starts with a kiosk — a clear, welcoming sign that says: here's where you are, here's what you'll find, here's how not to get lost. The first thing your students see should do the same. Right now, for most courses, that first thing is a 10-page document that reads like a legal contract. This module changes that.

The UDL Connection

First Impressions and Rejection Sensitivity

For students with Rejection Sensitivity Dysphoria (common in ADHD), first impressions matter enormously. A cold, rule-heavy syllabus signals: this class has a lot of ways to fail. A warm, clear splash page signals: this instructor sees you and wants you to succeed.

A well-designed course welcome page:

  • Reduces anxiety for students with ADHD, autism, and anxiety disorders
  • Supports executive function by surfacing the most critical information upfront
  • Communicates that your classroom is a structured and safe environment
  • Models the kind of accessible design we're asking students to navigate all semester
