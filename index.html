Welcome

Welcome to Base Camp.

This summer, CATL and Disability Resources are inviting you on a trail. The destination: a more accessible, flexible, neurodiversity-affirming course. The route: four practical tools, mapped to what your students actually told us they need.

You don't have to hike it all at once. You don't have to hike it alone.

Why This Matters

From Your Students

In December 2025, we sat down with neurodiverse students at SOU and asked them two direct questions: what helps you survive in a course? What helps you thrive?

Here's what they told us:

✓ What Works

  • Faculty who know their name
  • Instructors they can return to — repetition of the same faculty matters
  • "No-questions-asked" 48-hour extensions
  • Clear weekly structure and predictable course design

✗ What Blocks Them

  • Dense reading materials that screen readers can't parse
  • Being handed off to Disability Resources instead of having faculty engage directly
  • Rigid course structures that create barriers for students with different processing styles

These aren't accommodation requests. These are course design choices — and they're in your hands.

The Route

Four Trail Modules

This summer's experience is organized around four trail modules. Each one is connected to a specific tool and a specific learner need your students named.

Module 1

The Shared Map

Social Annotation with Hypothes.is

Students leave cairns for each other — annotating text together to surface key ideas and flag confusing passages.
Module 2

Clearing the Overgrowth

Brickfield Accessibility Toolkit

Audit your course for accessibility barriers and fix the fallen logs that are blocking your students' path.
Module 3

The Trailhead Kiosk

Gemini Syllabus Splash Page

Replace your 10-page syllabus contract with a welcoming course kiosk that tells students where they are and that you see them.
Module 4

The Scenic Overlook

Visual Signposting in Moodle

Consistent headers, icons, and structure so students spend cognitive energy on learning — not on decoding your course.
